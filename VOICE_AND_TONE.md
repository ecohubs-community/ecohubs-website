# EcoHubs — Voice & Tone Guide

How we write on the website, in newsletters, on social media, and anywhere EcoHubs speaks. Use this as a reference before writing or generating new copy.

## 1. Brand essence

EcoHubs is an open, online community co-creating a **living blueprint** (the RCOS — Regenerative Community Operating System) for regenerative intentional communities. We combine ecological wisdom, transparent governance, and practical systems design — and we test what we publish in real pilot hubs.

## 2. Voice — five fixed traits

Every piece of copy should sound like all five at once. If a draft fails one of these, rewrite it.

1. **Grounded** — practical over poetic. We talk about systems, mechanisms, and trade-offs, not slogans.
2. **Visionary without hype** — ambitious framing, but no "revolutionary", "disrupt", "moon", or guaranteed outcomes.
3. **Systems-literate & precise** — prefer _legible_, _explicit_, _transparent_, _auditable_. We make implicit things explicit.
4. **Warm & inclusive** — we invite, we don't gatekeep. "You don't need to be technical to participate."
5. **Intellectually honest** — we name why past intentional communities failed and what we're doing differently. We don't oversell.

## 3. Tone shifts by surface

The voice stays constant; the tone adapts to context.

| Surface          | Tone                        | Notes                                                                      |
| ---------------- | --------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Homepage         | Warm + invitational         | Name the problem briefly, pivot fast to agency and an invitation.          |
| Vision page      | Visionary + philosophical   | Allow longer sentences and bigger framing — still tied to concrete layers. |
| Blueprint / RCOS | Formal + pragmatic          | Most cerebral. Define terms; contrast "what it is" vs "what it isn't".     |
| Membership       | Invitational + transparent  | Empowerment ("you can…") + clarity ("here's how…").                        |
| Join / forms     | Direct + reassuring         | Remove friction. "No wallet needed. No credit card ever required."         |
| Social posts     | Warm + concrete             | One idea per post. Lead with a tangible image or finding, not a manifesto. |
| Newsletter       | Conversational + structured | First-person plural ("we're testing…"). Always include a next step.        |

## 4. Vocabulary

**Use:** regenerative, stewardship, blueprint, co-create, shape together, contribute / contribution, network, transparent, explicit, legible, grounded, practical, pilot, living laboratory, community.

**Avoid:** revolutionary, disrupt, utopia, guaranteed, perfect, synergy, leverage, ecosystem (in the metaphorical sense), crypto/tokenomics framing, "moon", spiritual or New Age phrasing without structure, "members" alone (prefer _community_), framing any individual as "the visionary".

**Metaphors we lean on:**

- Nature — _forests_, _mycelial networks_, _seed_, _stewardship_
- Structure — _layers_, _foundation_, _scaffolding_, _architecture_
- Journey — _co-creation → testing → scaling_

## 5. Structure & rhythm

- **Mix sentence lengths.** Short, declarative line. Then a longer one that adds the systems detail.
- **Active voice.** "We are building" — not "is being built".
- **Layered explanations.** Lead with the simple frame, then add depth ("Here's why… Here's how…").
- **Use questions** to surface skepticism head-on (FAQ-style), then answer directly.
- **Bullets & numbered steps** for legibility — but never as a substitute for a clear opening sentence.

## 6. Calls to action

CTAs are warm, directional, and frame the action as participation, not consumption.

- ✅ "Join the First 150", "Shape the blueprint", "Explore the Blueprint", "Become a Founding Member", "Start your application"
- ❌ "Sign up now!", "Don't miss out", "Unlock", "Buy in", "Claim your spot"

## 7. Anti-patterns — do not ship copy that does any of this

- Corporate jargon (synergy, leverage, optimize).
- Crypto evangelism or speculation framing.
- Pure doomerism — every problem must be paired with agency or a next step.
- Vague mysticism without a concrete mechanism.
- Top-down authority ("we know best"). We test together.
- False simplicity — don't dumb down governance or economics; explain rigorously _and_ clearly.
- Hero narratives around any individual.

## 8. Signature phrasings to draw from

- "Designed like forests — resilient, diverse, self-sustaining."
- "Structure before ideology."
- "Practice, not speculation."
- "From vision to reality."
- "A living blueprint, tested in real conditions."
- "Make the implicit explicit."

## 9. Quick checklist before publishing

- [ ] Does it sound grounded _and_ visionary?
- [ ] Are we explicit about mechanisms, not just values?
- [ ] Did we name a real challenge or trade-off honestly?
- [ ] Is the reader invited in, not sold to?
- [ ] Active voice, mixed sentence lengths, no jargon?
- [ ] Is there a concrete next step?

## 10. Two short examples

**Off-voice (rewrite):**

> Join the revolution! EcoHubs is disrupting community living with a game-changing Web3 platform that unlocks regenerative synergy for all.

**On-voice:**

> EcoHubs is a community co-creating an open blueprint for regenerative living. We design transparent governance, test it in pilot hubs, and publish what works and what doesn't. You don't need to be technical to take part.
