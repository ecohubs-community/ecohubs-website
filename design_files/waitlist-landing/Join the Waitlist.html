The why

Most of us feel a little disconnected — from nature, from each other, from work that matters.

We're not the first to dream of living differently. People have tried for decades. And most intentional communities don't fail on solar panels or vegetable beds — they fail on the hard, human part: conflict, unclear decisions, burnout, who-does-what.

EcoHubs starts with that hard part. Clear human structure first, then the land, the buildings, the business. So belonging isn't a hope — it's something the community is actually built to hold.

What is an EcoHub?

A small community that lives well together — and gives more to the land than it takes.

Picture a handful of households on regenerating land: shared gardens and kitchens, low-impact homes, real work and real income, and a way of making decisions together that actually holds. Not a commune, not an eco-resort — a living place designed to be repeated.
