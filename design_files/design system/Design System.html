01 · Principles

Five quiet rules
we keep returning to.

When something feels off, it usually broke one of these. We borrow more from a field journal than from a SaaS dashboard.

P1

Warm, not bright.

Backgrounds tilt toward ivory and unbleached paper. Pure white is reserved for cards that need to step forward.

P2

Two voices in the type.

Pridi for structural truth; Fraunces italic for the human aside. Inter holds everything in between.

P3

Pills, not rectangles.

Buttons are rounded-full. Cards round generously (20–28px). Hard corners feel imposed.

P4

Honest placeholders.

Missing imagery shows a striped sage placeholder with a mono caption — never a stock fill or AI render pretending to be real.

P5

Italics carry the feeling.

A Fraunces italic phrase inside a Pridi headline does almost all of the emotional work on this site. Use it where the sentence softens, qualifies, or admits something — not for emphasis in the SEO sense.

08 · Voice

Honest, slow,
a little tired of marketing.

We say

  • "You don't join EcoHubs. You start showing up for it."

  • "We'd rather undersell it than have you arrive disappointed."

  • "Free to join. No fee, no tier, no premium."

  • "Show up, or don't."

We don't say

  • "Unlock your regenerative journey today."

  • "Premium tier", "exclusive community", "limited spots"

  • "Join the movement" / "Be part of something bigger"

  • Hashtags, exclamation marks, emoji in body copy.

Three rules of thumb
  • Lowercase the verbs of urgency. "now" and "limited" do not appear.
  • Admit the unfinished. If something doesn't exist yet, say so. Pilot, prototype, draft are real words.
  • One italic phrase per paragraph, max. The italic is for where the voice softens, not where it sells.
